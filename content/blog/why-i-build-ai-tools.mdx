I didn't start building AI tools because it was trendy. I started because it was the first time I felt like the gap between an idea and a working thing was genuinely closable in a day.

Before LLMs, certain products required teams. Now they require one person with a clear head and enough context to prompt well. That's a different world.

## What I Actually Build

My projects aren't demos. They're products I use myself or build for real people:

- **Duxy** — an AI voice companion running in an Electron app
- **JotLabs** — a note-taking tool that builds a concept graph from your writing
- **Pluit** — cloud storage with a clean interface I actually want to use

Each one started from a frustration. Each one taught me something about how to make AI feel less like a chatbot and more like infrastructure.

## The Craft Part

The interesting technical challenges aren't the AI calls. They're the UX decisions around latency, around when to show thinking states, around how to make a response feel like it came from something that understood you rather than something that pattern-matched your words.

That's the part I'm obsessed with. And it's the part that's still wide open.
