---
title: "Why I Build in Public"
date: "2026-04-15"
excerpt: "The honest reason I put my work out there before it's ready."
tag: personal
---

Building in public is uncomfortable. You ship things that aren't finished, write about ideas that are half-formed, and let strangers watch you figure it out in real time.

That's exactly why I do it.

I've been building AI tools and web apps for a while now. Some of them are live. Some are still being assembled in the dark. But I've learned more from the ones I shipped early — rough edges and all — than from anything I polished in private.

When you build in public, you get feedback you didn't know you needed. You find people who care about the same problems. You create a record of your thinking that compounds over time.

The fear of being judged for imperfect work is a luxury that slows you down. Ship the thing. Write about the process. Let the iteration happen in front of people.

That's what Hixon.Studio is — a living record of what I'm building and why.
